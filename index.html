How It Works

Orthographic View

Elements are viewed straight-on with no perspective distortion. Lighting is calculated based on a virtual light source position.

Two-Light Setup

A key light provides strong highlights and shadows. A fill light softens shadows for natural illumination.

Elevation

Controls how high an element appears above the surface, creating a drop shadow that responds to light direction.

Edge Shapes

Three edge styles create different light interactions: fillet (rounded), chamfer (beveled), and groove (inset).

Thickness

Simulates material depth by adding a shadow on the opposite side of the light source.

Emitter

Elements that emit light glow outward, with customizable color and intensity.

Finishes

Surface finish affects specular reflection: matte absorbs light, gloss reflects it sharply.
